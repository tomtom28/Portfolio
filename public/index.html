About

Greetings! My name is Thomas Thompson and I am a graduate of the Rutgers School of Engineering and a current student at the Rutgers Coding Bootcamp.

This is my story. While working in operations at Amazon.com, I developed a growing interest in computer science that has since turned into a full-blown passion. And this past summer, with two years of process improvement under my belt, I decided to take the plunge and immerse myself in programming. My goal is to layer my newfound software skillset on top of my engineering and operations background to develop business intelligence dashboards and inventory management systems.

The specific software development skills in my toolbelt include: JavaScript, jQuery, Node.js, Express, HTML5/CSS3, Bootstrap, Materialize, Git, Responsive Design, API/JSON, RESTful API, Firebase, MySQL, MongoDB, Ajax, and Database Theory.

In addition to the fullstack web development skills, I have programming experience in Excel VBA, Matlab, and familiarity with C. Please check out some of my projects in the portfolio link! Note that some of this website is still under construction so please be sure to come back in the future to see how my portfolio progresses!

Eat Da Burger

A Node, Express, Handlebars, and MySQL burger app that lets users input the names of burgers they'd like to eat... and then devour them. HackOrg: QR Based Admissions

This application was an MLH Finalist Medal at HackNJIT 2016. It is a Node.js, Express, Handlebars, and MongoDB application that was built with the intention of helping streamline the admissions process at college Hackathon events. Students sign up to an event by creating an account using their email address, password, and a photo. At log-in, a unique QR Code is displayed for them to use at admission to the event and as a food voucher.

Bucket Buddiez

A web app that allows users to input places they would like to travel and then match with other users who also want to go there. On the Front End, the app uses Materialize, jQuery, AJAX, and the HTML is rendered using Handlebars. On the Back End, the app uses Node.js and MySQL. The Node Packages used in the app are Sequelize for database queries, Express for serving and routing, Passport for User Authenication, and bcrypt-nodejs for hashing user passwords.

News Paper, Web Scraper

A Node.js and MongoDB application that web-scrapes news data from The Onion and allows users to comment about what they have read. Users can also delete unwanted comments. On the Back End, the app uses Express to serve routes and Mongoose to interact with a MongoDB database. On the Front End, the app uses Handlebars for templating each article and Materialize as a styling framework. The app also uses jQuery and AJAX to help with making post requests. And for webscraping, the app uses the request and cheerio Node packages.
